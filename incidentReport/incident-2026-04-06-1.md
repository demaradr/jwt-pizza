# Incident: 2026-04-06 08-27-20

## Summary

Between the scheduled chaos window of **08:00** and **15:00** on **2026-04-06**, the instructor triggered Pizza Factory chaos event did not occur for my deployment. To complete the same operational objectives, I ran a my own chaos drill that reproduced the expected symptoms: elevated order latency, increased pizza purchase failures, and alert activity in Grafana OnCall.

The synthetic event was triggered by parallel curl traffic and large (20+ item) orders to stress the Pizza Factory dependency. This produced measurable degradation and fired the alert rules configured for latency and failures. I acknowledged the alerts, investigated metrics/logs, stopped the synthetic load, and verified recovery. No code changes were required during mitigation.


## Detection

The incident simulation was detected by "Latency" and "Pizza purchases" Grafana alert rules and delivered through Grafana OnCall. 

- Latency alerts moved from Normal -> Pending -> Firing after sustained load.
- Failure alerts based on increase(pizza_creation_failures_total[10m]) fired once factory calls began failing under stress.

Time to detect was a few minutes because of metric export interval, and alert evaluation interval.

## Impact

For approximately 45-60 minutes during the synthetic run, the service experienced:

- Increased response time on order endpoints.
- Intermittent order failures from the Pizza Factory path.
- Elevated HTTP POST traffic and temporary alert noise.

1,000,000 social media post were posted, we basically went viral. Just kidding, no real customers were affected

## Response

After receiving the first OnCall page, I acknowledged the incident and used dashboards to validate that the alert conditions were real and tied to current traffic. I identified load induced factory degradation as the active cause, and mitigated by terminating synthetic traffic.

This response mirrored the expected real chaos workflow which is acknowledge, triage, verify scope, apply operational mitigation, and confirm recovery.

## Root cause

The observed failures were caused by high concurrency and large orders stressing the external Pizza Factory integration. This was not an application code defect introduced during the incident window.

## Resolution

Service health was restored by ending synthetic load generation. Recovery was confirmed by:

- Order success rate returning to normal.
- increase(pizza_creation_failures_total[10m]) dropping back toward zero after the window elapsed.
- Latency expressions returning below alert thresholds.
- OnCall incidents returning to resolved/normal state.

## Prevention

Plan for capacity targets for peak order volume, enable horizontal autoscaling, and protect dependencies with concurrency limits and timeouts. Keep alerts focused on user-impact signals so scaling decisions happen early instead of after failures start.

## Action items

1. Update pizza failure alert to increase(pizza_creation_failures_total[10m]) > 0 so the rule clears correctly after incidents.
2. Add strict timeout on factory call, don’t let requests hang.
3. Keep latency alerts based on averages and tune thresholds from observed baseline plus test spikes.
4. add rate limiting or concurrency caps to protect service and factory from bursts.
